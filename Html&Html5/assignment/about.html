Definition: Photography, derived from the Greek words "photo" (light) and "graphy" (drawing or writing), literally means "drawing with light". Process: It involves using a camera to focus light reflected or emitted from objects onto a light-sensitive surface (film or a digital sensor) during a timed exposure. Applications: Art: Photography can be used as a medium for artistic expression, capturing emotions, telling stories, and exploring different perspectives. Documentation: It's used to record events, places, and objects, preserving moments in time. Communication: Photography plays a crucial role in mass communication, journalism, and advertising, conveying information and ideas visually.
